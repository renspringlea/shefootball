## Take-home message  

Factors that cost us goals during our R2 vs Noarlunga, and which I can improve:  
- The quality of my 1v1 finish  
- Commanding outfielders to fill gaps in defense  
- The quality of my goal kick  

Noarlunga's season to date is hit and miss. They've won or drawn every match, but many of those wins had a goal difference of 1. Their past 3 matches have all had a goal difference between 1 and 3, which suggests they might have dropped off compared to their earlier 6-0 and 7-0 matches. This means that Noarlunga can be beaten.  

The most prolific players are #4 and #15, who together have scored half of all Noarlunga's goals. However, Noarlunga has 12 separate players who have scored during the season, so of course we need to respect every player as a threat.  

## Goals we conceded in R2  

In round 2, Noarlunga beat us 7-0. Here is a brief description of all 7 goals. Numbers (#1 etc) refer to Noarlunga's shirt numbers.  
- Goal 1 (#1, 4min): #1 dispossessed us in midfield, turned and dribbled around our defenders, then shot from the top of the box. I didn't really attempt to save. Ball went in at near post.  
- Goal 2 (#4, 18min): #17 standing in the centre circle, made a through-ball to #4 at #4's centre-left. Defenders dropped off rather than chasing. #4 dribbled to the corner of the 6-yard box and shot. I attempted to save, but it wasn't very good. Ball went in at near post.  
- Goal 3 (#4, 30min): A midfielder, standing on the halfway line in about the middle, made a through-ball to #4 down the centre of the pitch. Our centre-defender was forward and there were no defenders between the two full-backs, leaving a huge gap. #4 dribbled the ball to the top of the box and shot along the ground. I dove to my left and actually got a finger to it, but the ball rolled in at the back post.  
- Goal 4 (#4, 68min): I took a goal kick on my right. It only just cleared the six-yard box (shocking!). Our right-back received, but got dispossessed by #4. #4 dribbled to the centre of the box (to my right, her left). She shot low and to the near post (my right). I was slow to dive and didn't get the ball in time, and it went in at the near post.  
- Goal 5 (#1, 74min): About 10 yards from the top of the box, on my right (her left), #4 made a curving cross/through-ball. It cleared 3 defenders (!), who were bunched up - there was a gap in the middle. I closed down #1, who took a touch around me (to my right) then easily slotted the ball in from the penalty spot. I probably could've dived and grabbed the ball from her feet!  
- Goal 6 (#4, 78min): A defender, standing just past the halfway line in their defensive half, passed to #4, who had beaten our defenders (who were probably sitting a bit too high). #4 received the ball about 10 yards into our attacking half. Lamb dribbled to the top of the box, beating our right-back. #4 shot from the middle six of the box. I closed down and actually deflected the ball, but it fell to #4 who tapped it in. My spread wasn't fantastic though!  
- Goal 7 (#3, 79min): After some ping pong, #3 received the ball about 10 yards past the top of the box. She dribbled past our left-back and shot from the top of the 6-yard box. The ball went in at the near post - I didn't really attempt to save.  

Every goal is a team goal, but there are some obvious errors on my part here. For each goal, the single action that I could've taken differently are as follows:  
- The quality of my 1v1 finish (4 goals)  
- Commanding outfielders to fill gaps in defense (2 goals)  
- The quality of my goal kick (1 goal)
