title: "4. Identifying ways to improve my goal kick technique using frame-by-frame analysis"    
---    

In [an earlier analysis]({% post_url 2026-05-12-goal-kicks %}), I showed that goal kicks (kicks from a dead ball off the ground) average 22 metres up the pitch while my punts (kicks out of my hands during open play) average 49 metres.  

In physics terms, there isn't any reason why there should be such a disparity. Kicking out of my hands provides a small advantage because the distance above the ground provides a small amount of extra height → longer duration in the air → further horizontal movement. There is also a small advantage that I take punts from ~10-12 metres higher up the pitch than goal kicks. But this advantage should be small — my goal kicks should be a little bit shorter than my punts, not less than half as long.  

The answer is that my goal kick technique isn't optimal.  

I recorded myself taking goal kicks from several angles using a high frame rate. I also downloaded videos of high-level players taking goal kicks or free kicks. This let me compare my own technique to these high-level players frame-by-frame. Since I'm right-footed, I used footage of other right-footed players.  

The key differences are:  
- The maximum height of my right leg in windup — this needs to increase, i.e. I need to windup that right leg more.  
- Body shape on contact — I need to hold my arms out for balance, lean back, and lean to the left.  
- Foot position on contact — I need to strike the ball with the top edge of the laces (first metatarsal bone), rather than the side of foot. This would be easier with the correct body shape.  

There are also a couple of things I'm already doing (on Samuel's advice) which are correct:  
- Starting position behind the ball — a few steps back, two steps to the side.  
- Left foot placement upon contact — a ball's width to the left of the ball.  

The following images show the comparison.  

For the windup of the right leg immediately before contact, notice how the high-level players bring the leg as high as it will go — their heel is almost touching their body. In comparison, my leg is bent at an angle of only 90 degress.
